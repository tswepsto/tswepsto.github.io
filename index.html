Design Sprint

I have a 5 step process for making applications that focuses on making a prototype and testing with customers. It's kind of like fast-forwarding into the future so you can see how people react before you go through all of that time and expense of releasing a design to your customers and viewers. I work with my customers during this process to ensure customer delight and project success. For things that are more simple like websites, I make them fairly quickly with high quality so that I can iterate with customers quickly.

Storyboarding

For each major project I work on, I facilitate a design sprint. Each sprint results in a tested prototype that acts as our guide for implementation. This image shows a storyboard that is created during the sprint that we create the prototype from. The prototype we build is then tested with 5 end users on the last day of our sprint.

Affinity Mapping

During user testing, participants of the design sprint create notes on sticky notes. We then put them on the whiteboard and categorize those comments to come up with fixes for our prototype before running the next sprint. This process yeilds and affinity map shown in this image.

Usability Testing

While working on applications and websites, I like to hold doing usability testing sessions. If there's no usability testing, then there's no usability design. You can reference best practices, but if you want to try something that is different that may be of higher risk, a great way to find out if you actually want to spend time and money on it is to find out if users and clients like your product. The return on investment for using UX design for your product is 300%. That's more than any other improvement that you can make to a product. It works great if you hold usability tests while designing, and after your application gets put into the market place.
